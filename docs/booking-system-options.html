Expert Booking System — Options

How customers will book a consultation with our experts: see availability, pick an open time slot, and get a meeting link automatically — no account needed, and protected from spam.

Prepared June 2026 · For review with our associate · Prices verified June 2026

What we're building

A simple "Book a Consultation" page on the website where a customer chooses an expert, sees that expert's open time slots for a chosen date, books one, and instantly receives a video-meeting link. The expert and customer both get a calendar invite and reminders — automatically.

Important design note: customers will see open slots only — never other people's bookings (that's private). Booked slots simply disappear from the grid. We (and each expert) see the full schedule on our side. Same goal, done the privacy-safe way.

We will not build a booking engine from scratch — storing availability, preventing double-bookings, generating meeting links, sending reminders and blocking spam are exactly what dedicated tools already do for free or cheap. We simply connect one to a beautifully branded page on our site.

How we keep it spam-proof

All three tools support the same layered protection — no open form for bots to abuse:

Always on

Layer 1 Email verification — a booking isn't confirmed until the customer verifies their email. Fake addresses die instantly.

Layer 2 Captcha (hCaptcha / reCAPTCHA) on the booking form to block bots.

Layer 3 Booking limits — minimum notice (e.g. 4 hours ahead), buffers between sessions, a cap on bookings per email per day, and a max future window.

Strongest (optional)

Layer 4 Small deposit / payment to book (even a token amount, refundable or credited to a purchase). This essentially eliminates spam and no-shows.

A natural fit if consultations are paid anyway. All three options support taking payment at booking via Stripe.

How it lives on the website

Whichever engine we choose, the customer experience on our site is the same:

  1. A new branded page — sahhadaily.com/pages/book ("Book a Consultation") — matching our green & Playfair design.
  2. It reuses our existing expert cards, each with a "Book a session" button (this is the expert filter — pick an expert).
  3. Clicking opens that expert's live calendar (inline or popup) → choose date → choose an open slot → name + email → confirm.
  4. Both sides get a calendar invite with the meeting link + automatic reminders.
  5. We add "Book" to the main menu and a button on the existing Experts section.

Does it integrate with Shopify?

Yes — all three sit natively on our site, and the customer never has to leave sahhadaily.com. Here's how each one connects:

Cal.com → embed code

We drop Cal.com's embed (inline calendar or popup button) into a branded Shopify section. We control both the page and the widget's styling. The customer books on-site; the Meet link and reminders fire automatically.

Google Workspace → embed code

Google Calendar gives each appointment page an "add to your website" snippet (a Book button or inline calendar). We paste it into our Shopify section — no plug-in required. The surrounding page is fully ours; the calendar widget itself loads in a Google iframe (lightly styleable — accent colour only).

Cowlendar → native app

Installed straight from the Shopify App Store and managed entirely inside Shopify admin. It adds its own booking popup — the deepest native integration, with no embed code to maintain.

Good to know

The "Google & YouTube" Shopify app is a different thing — it's for ads / Merchant Center / Analytics, not booking. For booking, the Google Calendar embed above is the route.

Branding control: Cal.com (full) › Google Workspace (page ours, widget Google-styled) › Cowlendar (their popup).

Our recommendation

It comes down to one question — do the experts already use (or want) professional Google email?

Already paying for / want Google Workspace email (@sahhadaily.com)? → Google Workspace Appointment Scheduling
Want strictly $0 and don't need Workspace? → Cal.com (free, per-expert accounts)
Want everything inside Shopify admin and OK with ~$15–30/mo? → Cowlendar (Shopify app)
Plain-language pick: if cost is the priority and we don't mind a one-time setup per expert, Cal.com is $0 and the most flexible. If we're setting up proper @sahhadaily.com emails anyway, Google Workspace gives us booking for free as a bonus and is the smoothest Google experience. Cowlendar is the easiest to run day-to-day, for a modest monthly fee.

Decisions we need to make

  1. Which engine — Cal.com (free) · Cowlendar (paid app) · Google Workspace.
  2. Free or paid consultations — paid doubles as the strongest anti-spam.
  3. Video + calendar — Google Meet (simplest) or Zoom; which do the experts use?
  4. Reminders — email is enough, or do we want SMS/WhatsApp too?
  5. How many experts to launch with, and their availability hours.
